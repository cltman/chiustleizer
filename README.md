# CHIUSTLEIZER

**An industrial tone unit for your browser. Hand built, spiritually, in 1977.**

🎛️ **[▶ PLAY IT LIVE](https://cltman.github.io/chiustleizer/)** — no install, no plugins, just noise.

## What is this?

A playable synthesizer built around the legendary **Gristleizer** circuit — Roy Gwinn's
"Guitar Effects Pedal" from *Practical Electronics* (July 1975), famously modified by
Chris Carter and used on virtually everything Throbbing Gristle ever recorded.

The original was an effect you fed sound into. This one generates its own: a polyphonic
oscillator and keyboard feed the classic Gristleizer chain — a morphing drive stage,
then either a **VCA** (tremolo → hard gate → ring modulation) or a screaming swept
**VCF** bandpass filter, all wobbled by an LFO with four waveshapes.

## Features

- 2-octave keyboard, mouse or QWERTY playable (`Z`/`X` shift octaves ±2)
- 4 oscillator waves · 4 LFO shapes · LFO from one cycle per 20 s up to 120 Hz
- Drive stage that morphs from warm tape-ish clipping into an asymmetric wavefolder
- Resonant filter with up to +24 dB of self-overloading scream
- Arpeggiator: UP / DOWN / UP-DN / RND, scheduled on the audio clock
- **33 built-in programs**, including recreations of classic Throbbing Gristle
  territory (*Hamburger Lady Vocal Fuzz*, *Duck Call 1978*, *Drum Box Gate*)
- Pulsing LFO lamp, Dymo labels, corner screws. The felt strip is red, as it should be.

## Run it locally

Download `Chiustleizer.html` and double-click it. That's the whole install.
